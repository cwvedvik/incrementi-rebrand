01 — Executive summary

One brand. One conviction. The whole journey.

Incrementi stops being "an IT company that also does AI" and becomes the category-defining partner that builds the operating system beneath an operationally-intensive firm — and hands its people the ability to run it.

Incrementi — part of 99X Group — already wins serious enterprise mandates for data platforms, digital products and AI (ABYS, NRC Group, Sebastian, Optimar, Maritech). But the brand today undersells the ambition: it reads as a capable IT partner, not the category leader it intends to become.

This strategy repositions Incrementi as a single, deliberately premium, AI-led brand: the partner that owns the entire transformation to AI-native — from data foundation to employees building their own agents — for industrial, maritime and financial firms across the Nordics.

02 — Where we play & why now

The whitespace between advisors and vendors.

Operationally-intensive firms are caught between three inadequate options. Incrementi is built to be the fourth — and the only one that owns the whole journey.

Strategy houses

Advise, then leave

Big-name consultancies produce roadmaps and decks. The build — and the value — never arrives. The client is left to execute alone.

Software vendors

Lock you in

Off-the-shelf platforms own your data and your roadmap. Every new need becomes a feature request and a renewal negotiation.

Dev shops & AI startups

Ship features, not foundations

Point solutions and chatbots bolted on top of messy data. Impressive demos, fragile architecture, mounting technical debt.

Incrementi

Build it, and hand it over

Own the journey end-to-end: foundation, platform, AI context layer, and the capability for the firm to run and extend it themselves. You own the IP.

Why now

  • AI has outrun most firms' foundations. Everyone wants agents; almost no one has the governed data context to run them safely. That gap is the market.
  • Delivery economics have inverted. AI-assisted development (AI-DLC) makes it possible to deliver enterprise software faster and cheaper than the incumbents — without lowering quality.
  • The Nordic mid-market is underserved. Industrial, maritime and financial firms are too operationally specific for generic AI vendors and too pragmatic for big-five theatre.
The name is the strategy

Incrementi — increments that compound. Every data source connected, every module shipped, every agent built makes the next one faster, cheaper and more powerful.

This isn't a slogan. It's documented in the work: at NRC Group, a new HR-system integration landed in under half the original estimate because the platform was already there. Advantage that compounds is the brand's central promise.

08 — Brand identity & voice

Dark, editorial, exclusive — and human.

Premium enough for the boardroom of a listed company; warm and clear enough that it never reads as a cold, expensive agency. This very document is built in the proposed system.

Colour

A near-black canvas with warm off-white type and a single refined metallic copper accent. Cool steel as a secondary accent for data/technical contexts. Copper is used sparingly: it should feel like brass on dark wood, not a highlighter.

Ink#0A0B0D · canvas
Deep Navy#0E1622 · alt canvas
Copper#C98A5E · accent
Steel#8FA3B8 · secondary
Paper#F4F2EE · text

Typography

A serif with a point of view,
set against a quiet grotesque.
Display in Fraunces (an editorial, high-contrast serif) signals craft, confidence and intelligence — the "premium magazine" register. Body and interface in DM Sans keep it modern, legible and approachable. Numbers and data lean on the same pairing.
Labels · uppercase · wide tracking · DM Sans

Both families are open-source (Google Fonts) — no licensing cost, web-safe, and load fast. Fraunces replaces the all-DM-Sans look and is the single biggest visual lift from the current brand.

Imagery

Cinematic & real

High-contrast, dark-graded photography of real industrial and maritime environments — vessels, decks, control rooms, machinery. Abstract architecture/data visuals as texture. Always lots of negative space. Never stock-photo "AI" clichés.

Layout & motion

Editorial restraint

Generous margins, strong grid, large type, thin rules instead of heavy dividers. Motion is slow and deliberate — quiet reveals, no bounce. Confidence is shown by what we leave out.

Logo

Kept, framed better

The Incrementi wordmark stays as-is. We define clear-space, dark/light lockups, and a 99X-endorsement lockup. The new system makes the existing logo look more expensive without redrawing it.

Voice — English, Scandinavian backbone

  • Confident, never loud. We state outcomes plainly and let proof carry the weight.
  • Precise and concrete. Specific numbers, real systems, named sectors — no vague "transformation" fog.
  • Human and direct. Short sentences. Active voice. We explain, we don't posture.
  • Premium, not corporate. Editorial warmth over consulting jargon. We sound like the smartest person in the room who has nothing to prove.

11 — The web experience

The website is a living intelligence.

This is where the rebrand becomes unforgettable. incrementi.no is not a brochure you scroll — it's a mind you converse with. The medium is the message: we build the brain for a firm, so our site is a brain. (Positioning and messaging are unchanged — this changes only how they're delivered.)

At the centre sits a living neural organism — Incrementi's intelligence, breathing, pulsing, reacting. Instead of a wall of copy, the visitor is invited to ask. Suggested questions surround a single chat input ("How do you take a firm to AI-native?", "Do we own what you build?", "Show me the proof"). The intelligence reasons, then reveals only the content that visitor wants — the right messaging, on demand.

It embodies every pillar at once: an AI-native experience, grounded in context, that surfaces exactly what matters. Two states carry it: an immersive hero where the organism is centre-stage, and a conversation where it recedes to a living presence while answers stream in. Sub-pages (The Journey, What we build, Industries, Work, About) remain fully clickable — each simply asks the intelligence a question.

Reference point: Manta (manta.eu) proves the "built like a brain" register works at the premium end. We adapt it to Incrementi's industrial/maritime world and the compounding-advantage idea — warmer, copper-on-navy, and centred on conversation rather than a static graph.

Why it wins
  • Unforgettable & ownable — no Nordic competitor has anything close.
  • Self-qualifying — the questions a visitor asks reveal their intent and segment.
  • Premium by feel — restraint, motion and depth do the work; it never reads as a loud agency.
  • The proof is the product — a firm that sells intelligence, demonstrated the moment you land.
Build notes

Canvas/WebGL organism, pre-authored answer library at launch, with a clear path to wire it to a real, governed model later (a live demo of our own context layer). Full accessibility fallback: every answer is a real, crawlable page underneath, so SEO and screen-readers are never sacrificed for spectacle. A working concept prototype accompanies this document.
